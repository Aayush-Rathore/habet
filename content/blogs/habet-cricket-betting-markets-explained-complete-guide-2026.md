## Understanding Cricket Betting Odds {#odds}

### Decimal Odds Explained

HABET uses decimal odds — the most common format in India. Understanding them is essential before betting on any market.

**Formula:** Profit = (Odds × Stake) - Stake

**Examples:**
- Odds of 2.00: Bet ₹1,000 → Win ₹1,000 profit (total return ₹2,000)
- Odds of 1.50: Bet ₹1,000 → Win ₹500 profit (total return ₹1,500)
- Odds of 3.50: Bet ₹1,000 → Win ₹2,500 profit (total return ₹3,500)

### Implied Probability

Odds represent probability. Convert odds to probability with this formula:

**Implied Probability = (1 / Decimal Odds) × 100**

- Odds 2.00 = 50% implied probability
- Odds 1.67 = 60% implied probability
- Odds 3.00 = 33.3% implied probability

**Finding value:** If you believe a team has a 60% chance of winning but HABET's odds imply only 50%, you have a value bet. Consistently finding value is the foundation of profitable cricket betting.

## Pre-Match Markets {#pre-match}

### Match Winner (Moneyline)

The simplest and most popular market — you pick which team wins the match.

**How it works:**
- Two-way market for T20 and ODI matches (no draw possible)
- Three-way market for Test matches (Team A wins / Draw / Team B wins)
- Odds reflect each team's probability of winning

**Example IPL odds:**
- Mumbai Indians vs Chennai Super Kings
- MI: 1.85 | CSK: 2.05
- Implied: MI 54% | CSK 49% (total 103% = 3% margin)

**Best strategy:** Focus on home team advantage (58% win rate in IPL), recent form (last 5 matches), and toss result for day-night matches.

### Toss Winner

Bet on which team wins the coin toss. Odds are typically 1.90/1.90 (approximately 50/50 with a small margin).

**Why it matters:** In IPL day-night matches, the toss winner who chooses to field first wins 54% of matches due to dew in the second innings. Toss winner markets are useful for live betting strategy.

### Total Runs (Over/Under)

Bet on whether the total runs scored in the match will be above or below a set line.

**Example:**
- Total runs line: 330.5
- Over 330.5: 1.90 | Under 330.5: 1.90

**Key factors:**
- Venue: Wankhede (Mumbai) averages 185 total runs per innings; Chepauk (Chennai) averages 158
- Pitch report: "Good batting surface" = higher totals; "Two-paced surface" = lower totals
- Weather: Dew in second innings increases second-innings scoring

### First Innings Runs

Bet on the total runs scored in the first innings only.

**Example:**
- First innings runs line: 165.5
- Over 165.5: 1.85 | Under 165.5: 1.95

This market is useful when you have strong views on one team's batting but uncertainty about the second innings.

### Innings Runs (Specific Overs)

Bet on runs scored in specific over ranges:
- **Powerplay runs (overs 1-6):** Typically 45-65 runs in IPL
- **Middle overs (7-15):** Typically 60-80 runs
- **Death overs (16-20):** Typically 50-70 runs

**Strategy:** Teams with strong powerplay batsmen (like Rohit Sharma, KL Rahul) tend to score 55+ in the powerplay. Teams with proven death-overs hitters (like Hardik Pandya, Andre Russell) tend to score 65+ in overs 16-20.

## Player Performance Markets {#player-markets}

### Top Batsman

Bet on which player will score the most runs in the match (or innings).

**Example IPL odds:**
- Virat Kohli (RCB): 4.50
- Rohit Sharma (MI): 5.00
- Shubman Gill (GT): 6.00
- KL Rahul (LSG): 7.00

**Strategy:** Top batsman markets are less efficient than match winner markets. Focus on:
- Recent form (last 5 innings average)
- Venue performance (some batsmen have strong records at specific grounds)
- Pitch conditions (flat pitches favor aggressive batsmen; slow pitches favor technically sound batsmen)
- Opposition bowling attack (check if the bowlers are strong against left-handers or right-handers)

### Top Bowler

Bet on which player will take the most wickets in the match.

**Example IPL odds:**
- Jasprit Bumrah (MI): 4.00
- Yuzvendra Chahal (RR): 5.50
- Mohammed Siraj (RCB): 6.00

**Strategy:** Bowler performance is heavily pitch-dependent. Chahal's leg-spin is devastating on dry, turning pitches (economy 7.2) but expensive on flat surfaces (economy 9.1). Always check the pitch report before backing a spinner.

### Player Runs (Individual)

Bet on whether a specific player will score over or under a set number of runs.

**Example:**
- Virat Kohli over/under 35.5 runs: 1.85/1.95

**Strategy:** Compare the line to the player's recent average. If Kohli is averaging 45 in his last 5 innings but the line is set at 35.5, the over offers value.

### Player Wickets (Individual)

Bet on whether a specific bowler will take over or under a set number of wickets.

**Example:**
- Jasprit Bumrah over/under 1.5 wickets: 1.75/2.05

**Strategy:** Bumrah averages 1.8 wickets per IPL match. The over at 1.5 wickets offers value when he's bowling on a pitch that suits his style (hard, bouncy surfaces).

### Man of the Match

Bet on which player will be awarded Man of the Match.

**Example odds:**
- Virat Kohli: 6.00
- Rohit Sharma: 7.00
- Jasprit Bumrah: 8.00

**Strategy:** Man of the Match is typically awarded to the player with the biggest individual impact. In high-scoring matches, top batsmen win more often. In low-scoring matches, bowlers win more often.

## Betting Strategies by Market Type {#strategies}

### Strategy 1: The Powerplay Momentum Play

**Market:** Match Winner (Live)
**Trigger:** Team scores 55+ in the powerplay

Research across 500+ IPL matches shows teams scoring 55+ in the powerplay win 68% of matches. When a team reaches 55+ in 6 overs, their live odds often still reflect only 55-60% probability — undervaluing their actual 68% chance.

**Execution:**
1. Monitor the powerplay score
2. When a team reaches 55+ in 6 overs, check the live match winner odds
3. If the batting team's odds are 1.80 or higher (implied 55% or less), bet on them
4. Expected value: 68% win rate at 1.80 odds = 22.4% positive expected value

### Strategy 2: The Pitch Report Arbitrage

**Market:** Total Runs (Over/Under)
**Trigger:** Venue-specific scoring patterns

Different pitches produce dramatically different scoring patterns:
- Wankhede (Mumbai): Average 185 total runs per innings
- Chepauk (Chennai): Average 158 total runs per innings
- Eden Gardens (Kolkata): Average 172 total runs per innings

When bookmakers set over/under lines without fully adjusting for venue, you can exploit the gap. Always check the official BCCI pitch report before placing over/under bets.

### Strategy 3: The Wicket Window Play

**Market:** Next Over Runs (Live)
**Trigger:** Overs 6-10 in T20 matches

The 6-10 over period in T20s often sees a cluster of wickets as batsmen try to accelerate after the powerplay. Bowling team odds frequently offer value here.

**Execution:**
1. Monitor the match from overs 6-10
2. When a wicket falls and a new batsman comes in, back the under on next over runs
3. New batsmen typically take 2-3 balls to settle, reducing scoring rate

### Strategy 4: The Dew Factor Play

**Market:** Match Winner (Live)
**Trigger:** Evening matches at dew-prone venues

In evening IPL matches, dew accumulates on the outfield from approximately over 12-15 of the second innings. This makes the ball slippery, reducing bowlers' grip and increasing scoring rates for the chasing team.

**Execution:**
1. Identify evening matches at dew-prone venues (Wankhede, Eden Gardens, Chinnaswamy)
2. If the chasing team is behind the required rate at over 10, their odds may still be generous
3. Back the chasing team before the dew effect fully adjusts the odds
