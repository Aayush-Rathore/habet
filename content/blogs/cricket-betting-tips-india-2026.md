### Match Winner (Moneyline)

The simplest market — you pick which team wins the match. For T20 matches, odds typically range from 1.50 to 3.50 depending on team strength and pitch conditions. In IPL 2025, the average odds for a top-four team winning at home were around 1.65, meaning a ₹1,000 bet returned ₹1,650.

### Top Batsman / Top Bowler

These prop markets let you bet on individual performances. They're popular because a single player's form can be easier to predict than a full team result. Look for batsmen averaging 45+ in recent T20s and bowlers with economy rates below 7.5 in the last five matches. ### Over/Under Runs

You bet on whether the total runs scored will be above or below a set line. For example, if the line is 165.5 runs in a T20, you bet "over" if you expect a high-scoring game. Pitch reports and weather conditions are critical here — a dry, turning pitch in Chennai typically produces 15–20 fewer runs than a flat Wankhede surface.

## Reading Cricket Odds Like a Pro

Odds represent probability. An odds of 2.00 implies a 50% chance of winning. An odds of 1.50 implies a 66.7% chance. The formula is simple:

**Implied Probability = 1 / Decimal Odds × 100**

If you believe a team has a 60% chance of winning but the odds imply only 50%, you have a value bet. Over hundreds of bets, consistently finding value is what separates profitable bettors from recreational ones.

## Live Betting Tips for Cricket

Live betting requires quick thinking and pre-match preparation. Here's how to approach it:

- **Set price alerts** — Know the odds at which you want to enter a market before the match starts.
- **Watch the first over** — The first over of a T20 innings reveals pitch pace and bounce. If the ball is coming on nicely, back the batting team on the over/under.
- **Wicket windows** — The 6–10 over period in T20s often sees a cluster of wickets as batsmen try to accelerate. Odds on the bowling team frequently offer value here.
- **Death overs specialist** — If a team has a proven death-overs bowler (economy under 8.5 in overs 16–20), back them on the "next over runs" market during the death.
