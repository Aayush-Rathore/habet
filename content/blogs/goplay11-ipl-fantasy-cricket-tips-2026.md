## Understanding the GoPlay11 Scoring System

Before building winning teams, you need to understand exactly how points are scored. See our [GoPlay11 app review](/blog/goplay11-app-review-fantasy-cricket-india-2026) for the full scoring breakdown. Key points for IPL strategy:

**T20 scoring priorities (highest points per match):**
1. **All-rounders** — Contribute with both bat and ball; can score 80–150+ points in a single match
2. **Top-order batsmen** — Score runs quickly in T20; 50+ runs = 8 bonus points
3. **Death bowlers** — Take wickets in overs 16–20; 3 wickets = 79 points
4. **Wicket-keepers** — Earn fielding points (stumping = 12 pts) plus batting points

**T20 scoring traps to avoid:**
- Middle-order batsmen who bat at 6–7 rarely get enough balls to score big
- Spinners who bowl only 2–3 overs have limited wicket-taking opportunities
- Tail-end batsmen almost never contribute meaningful batting points

## Team Selection Framework

### Step 1: Identify the Match Type

Before selecting players, classify the match:

**High-scoring match (expected total 180+):** Flat pitch, small ground, strong batting lineups. Prioritise top-order batsmen and all-rounders. Avoid specialist bowlers.

**Low-scoring match (expected total 150–165):** Slow/turning pitch, large ground, strong bowling lineups. Prioritise bowlers and all-rounders. Avoid lower-order batsmen.

**Balanced match (expected total 165–180):** Standard conditions. Balanced team selection.

For pitch analysis, check the BCCI pitch report 24 hours before the match. Our [cricket betting strategies guide](/blog/cricket-betting-strategies-india-2026) covers pitch analysis in detail — the same analysis applies to fantasy team selection.

### Step 2: Select Your Core Players (7–8 players)

These are high-probability performers you're confident about regardless of conditions:

**IPL 2026 core player candidates:**
- **Virat Kohli (RCB)** — Consistent top-order scorer; 8,000+ IPL runs; safe captain option at Chinnaswamy
- **Rohit Sharma (MI)** — Best powerplay hitter; safe pick when MI bat first at Wankhede
- **Jasprit Bumrah (MI)** — Best T20 bowler; safe pick in all conditions
- **Ravindra Jadeja (CSK)** — All-rounder; contributes with bat, ball, and fielding; excellent at Chepauk
- **Suryakumar Yadav (MI)** — Highest T20 strike rate among active Indian batsmen; safe pick in any batting position

### Step 3: Select Your Differential Players (3–4 players)

Differentials are players that most users won't pick but have high upside. In large contests, differentials are what separate winners from the pack.

**How to find differentials:**
- Look for players returning from injury who are underpriced
- Target players in good form who are batting higher than their usual position
- Find bowlers who are likely to bowl in the powerplay AND death overs (maximum overs = maximum wicket opportunities)
- Target players in the opposition's weak spot (e.g., a left-arm spinner against a team with multiple right-handed batsmen who struggle against spin)

**IPL 2026 differential candidates to watch:**
- Lower-order batsmen who have been promoted up the order
- Pace bowlers playing at venues where pace is effective (Wankhede, Eden Gardens)
- Spinners playing at Chepauk or Sawai Mansingh

### Step 4: Captaincy Selection

Captaincy is the most important decision in fantasy cricket. Your captain scores 2x points — a good captain pick can win you a contest; a bad one can cost you.

**Captain selection principles:**

**In high-scoring matches:** Pick the most consistent top-order batsman from the team expected to bat first. They get the most balls and the best conditions.

**In low-scoring matches:** Pick the best bowler from the team expected to bowl in favourable conditions. A 4-wicket haul from your captain = 33 × 2 = 66 points.

**In balanced matches:** Pick an all-rounder who contributes with both bat and ball. Ravindra Jadeja, Hardik Pandya, and Axar Patel are reliable all-round captain options.

**The differential captain strategy:** In large contests (100+ users), if 60% of users pick Virat Kohli as captain, you need a different pick to win. Even if Kohli scores 80 points, everyone else with Kohli as captain also gets 160 points. To win, you need a captain who outperforms expectations.

## Bankroll Management for GoPlay11

Fantasy cricket bankroll management is as important as team selection. Poor bankroll management is the #1 reason good fantasy players lose money.

### The 10% Rule

Never spend more than 10% of your total fantasy bankroll on a single match. If your bankroll is ₹1,000, your maximum spend per match is ₹100.

**Why:** Even the best fantasy players have losing streaks. A 5-match losing streak at 10% per match reduces your bankroll by 41% — painful but survivable. At 50% per match, the same streak wipes out 97% of your bankroll.

### The Contest Mix

For a ₹100 match budget:
- ₹50 in small/medium contests (higher win probability, lower variance)
- ₹30 in medium contests (balanced risk/reward)
- ₹20 in one mega contest (lottery ticket for big prize)

This mix gives you consistent small wins while maintaining exposure to large prizes.

## Common GoPlay11 Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Picking too many players from one team** — Maximum 7 from one team, but 5–6 is usually optimal for balance
2. **Ignoring the toss** — The toss determines batting order, which affects player selection. Wait for the toss before finalising your team if possible
3. **Picking injured players** — Always check team news 30 minutes before the match. An injured player scores 0 points
4. **Copying popular teams** — In large contests, popular teams don't win. You need differentiation
5. **Chasing losses** — After a bad match, don't increase your contest spend to recover. Stick to your bankroll rules
